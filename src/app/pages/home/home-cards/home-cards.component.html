Scaffolding

Scaffolding explores new methods of teaching the basic principles of programming. This phase focuses on linear problem solving and is supported by programming lab assignments (PLAs) during term time. Scaffolding data can be revealing of how well a student is grasping the taught concepts and to help teachers assess areas that require improvement.

Discovery

Discovery involves open or guided, non-linear problem-based learning with exploration to solve requirements. Discovery is encouraged with project work that forces students to independently research and consider alternatives to develop and evaluate their solutions. This is an iterative design approach based on prior experiences.

Innovation

Innovation builds upon the two previous phases. This phase pushes students to identify optimal solutions based on the knowledge gathered through scaffolding and discovery. Innovation is encouraged by giving students a larger problem which enables them to develop something innovative and exciting.

Constructionism - the underlying theory

SDI is based on constructionism, a learning theory which stems from constructivist principles in psychology. Constructivist theories hold a view of learning as reconstruction process based on the idea that learners construct mental models in other to reason about their world. Constructionist learning is a student-centred, iterative approach that promotes discovery learning i.e. students are encouraged to use information known a priori as an instrument to acquire more knowledge and to build increasingly complex levels of understanding. This is alternatively known as apprenticeship learning i.e. learn by doing and based on the principle that “the way teachers teach should be adapted to how the students learn”.
